Fighter Jets

Fighter aircraft (early on also called pursuit aircraft) are military aircraft designed primarily for air-to-air combat. In military conflict, their role is to establish control of the airspace above a battlefield. This allows bombers and attack aircraft to carry out missions while preventing enemy aircraft from doing the same. Key features of a fighter jet include high speed, strong maneuverability, and advanced weapons systems. The success of these aircraft depends on pilot skill, strategy, and the performance of the aircraft itself. Many modern fighters can also perform ground attacks, while others are specialized for specific roles such as interception or night operations.
