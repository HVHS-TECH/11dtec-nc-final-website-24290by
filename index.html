Lego Vacuume Engines

Lego vacuum engines are recreational models based on real combustion engines. Instead of running on fuel and oil, they are powered by a vacuum source. The system uses negative air pressure to create motion, where suction pulls an articulating piston upward inside a cylinder. To ensure the "Engine" Completes a full rotation, the design relies on a series of valves that open and close at specific times these valve concepts can be seen in real engines such as OHV, SVS and Occasionaly Side Valves. These systems allow air to move only in a single direction, As the vacuum pulls the piston up, air is selectively reintroduced into the chamber to reset the cycle, enabling continuous motion. The overall mechanism mimics the timing and coordination of real engines, with precise valve sequencing and airtight construction being essential for efficiency. By using LEGO components, the design becomes a creative demonstration of engine principles without any real combustion.
