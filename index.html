Lego Vacuume Engines

Lego vacuum engines are recreational models inspired by real combustion engines. Instead of running on fuel and oil, they are powered by a vacuum source. The system uses negative air pressure to create motion, where a controlled vacuum pulls an articulating piston upward inside a cylinder. To ensure the motion flows in one direction, the design relies on a series of one-way valves and carefully managed airflow paths. These valves allow air to move only in a single direction, preventing backflow and maintaining consistent pressure differences across the piston. As the vacuum pulls the piston up, air is selectively reintroduced or redirected to reset the cycle, enabling continuous motion. The overall mechanism mimics the timing and coordination of real engines, with precise valve sequencing and airtight construction being essential for efficiency. By using LEGO components, the design becomes a creative demonstration of engine principles without any real combustion.
